# E17 — Post-adoption D-class case mining (E13 method, wave-4 pool)

**Question**: after adopting `pad-lines=5` + `len-exp=0.85` (commit `5e81c8a`, PR #40), what does the *residual* D-class (right file, wrong function) pool look like, and which implementable signal covers the most of it?

**Method** (E13's, updated; read-only throughout — `git show`/`git archive` object-DB reads only, zero checkouts of `lab/swebench_repos` working trees, no eval runs):

1. **Stage 1 (screening):** per-instance D-mass taken directly from the committed E7b per-line classifications (`lab/research/wave4/data/miss_autopsy_raw_lite.json` = full300_v11, `miss_autopsy_raw_verified.json` = full407_verified_new) joined against the run jsonls for returned spans. 280 instances have D-mass > 0 (99 Lite, 181 Verified), totaling 4759 D-lines across both sets. Top 30 by D-mass: **26 Verified + 4 Lite** (the Lite four — `flask-5063`, `django-13265`, `sklearn-25500`, `sympy-20322` — are all E13/E7b repeat offenders), covering 1775 D-lines (37.3% of the combined pool; min D-mass in set = 34). The mining pool is now overwhelmingly held-out — conclusions here are no longer Lite-tuned.
2. **Stage 2 (feature battery, top 30):** each repo materialized at `base_commit` via `git archive | tar -x` into private scratch, real `lanes2.Corpus` rebuilt for true corpus-wide idf, and the **adopted** within-file objective recomputed exactly — Rust `python_blocks()` nested candidate spans, `gain = idf-weighted seg-term overlap + 0.5·hit-lines`, selection metric `gain / tok^0.85` (cl100k tokens; the per-file `(0.3 + score)` factor is constant within a file and drops out; `w_name=0` in production). Gold = AST-innermost owner of each D-line; chosen = highest-metric function overlapping any actually-returned span in the gold file. E13 features plus two new ones: (a) pad-reach (was gold within ≤5 lines of a chosen span), (b) sibling precondition (does gold's class contain a chosen span), plus a stage-2b **in-class rank** measurement (gold's rank among its class's own not-yet-chosen candidates — the honest efficacy bound for any sibling boost).

30/30 cases ran clean. 7/30 have a **module-level dominant owner** (no enclosing function), 23 are genuine function-vs-function contests.

## The pool's composition has changed in two structural ways

**1. Fragmentation.** E13's top-30 were *all* single-dominant-function cases. Now: **11/30 have no majority owner** (top function owns <50% of the file's D-mass) and **22/30 spread across ≥4 distinct owner functions**. Extreme case `sympy-13878` (D=127, the #1 case): the gold patch touches **18 sibling distribution constructors** (`Frechet`, `Arcsin`, `Dagum`, `StudentT`, `UniformSum`, …) in `crv_types.py` plus 21 module-level lines — there is no single "right function" to rank; capturing this instance means capturing a *sweep*. The padding+length-norm adoption consumed the concentrated single-function mass and left the scattered multi-site patches behind. Any single-pick fix has a hard per-instance ceiling on 11 of these 30.

**2. Module-level mass grew in relative terms.** 7/30 dominant owners are module-level (vs 4/30 in E13), 28/30 files have ≥1 module-level D-line, and module-level-owned lines are **252/1540 = 16%** of dominant-file D-mass (E13 measured 13.3% repo-wide). E13 and E7b both flagged the import/preamble force-include as near-free; it is *still unshipped* and its relative share keeps growing as function-level fixes consume the rest.

**New feature (a) — pad-reach: an honest null.** **0/30 files have even one D-line within 5 lines of a chosen span** (0 within 10 as well); median gold-function distance to the nearest chosen span is **57 lines**. Padding did not "almost catch" anything that remains — pad=5 already harvested everything within reach (that was B-class, per E7b), and the D-remnant is structurally beyond any sane pad radius. In 3/23 cases the chosen span *abuts the gold function's boundary* (distance 1–2: `sympy-12489`, `django-16263`, `sklearn-12682` — the engine picked the immediately-adjacent sibling function), but the D-lines sit deep inside a 100+-line gold body, so more padding would only nibble the signature. **More padding is a dead lever for D.**

**New feature (b) — sibling precondition: high coverage, but the naive boost under-delivers.** Gold is a method in 19/23 contests; **its class already contains a chosen span in 14/19 (74%)** — up sharply from E13's 42% same-class rate, and covering **51% of top-30 dominant-file D-mass**. The right class is being anchored more often than ever; the greedy pick then walks off to a sibling stub. *But* stage-2b's efficacy bound is sobering: among the sibling-covered cases, gold is the **#1 not-yet-chosen candidate within its own class in only 2/12** (≤3 in 4/12; ranks run 1,1,3,3,5,8,11,18,22,25,34,48). Big classes reproduce the whole-file pathology fractally — `Query` has 86 in-class candidates, `Dataset` 180 — so "boost siblings of an anchored class" with the *same density metric* would seat the right sibling in only ~⅙ of covered cases. Precondition coverage (51% of D-mass) is an upper bound, not an expected yield; a realistic naive-boost yield is ~9–17% of contests plus partial line capture.

## The length-exponent lever is exhausted (closes E7b's recommendation #2)

For each contest, the exponent `e` at which gold's `gain/tok^e` would overtake the top chosen (pairwise, optimistic — ignores the other ~40 competitors): **not a single top-30 case flips at e=0.7 or even 0.6**. Every flippable case needs **e ≤ 0.57** (distribution: 0.56, 0.55, 0.54, 0.53×4, 0.50, 0.48×3, 0.47×2, 0.44, 0.36, 0.30, 0.28, 0.27, 0.25, 0.15, 0.12), and 3/23 flip at *no* positive exponent (`flask-5063`, `sklearn-25500` — gold shorter *and* lower-gain; `matplotlib-26466` — gold's gain is absolutely lower). Cross-checked against the already-run gates: E14's len-exp-only arms scored FUNCTION 45.67 (0.55) / 44.00 (0.70) / 44.33 (0.85), and the **combo p5+l055 regressed to 47.67 FUNCTION / 0.483 fraction vs the adopted p5+l085's 53.33 / 0.517**. The mining explains the gate: the remaining pool needs e≈0.5 or lower, and the aggregate already showed that costs far more elsewhere (the two gold-shorter reversal cases here are the visible tip of that cost). **E7b's "exponent is under-damped" hypothesis is falsified for the residual pool — the same knob cannot be turned further; the remaining D-mass needs a different mechanism.**

## Five vignettes

**1. `sympy__sympy-13878`** (Verified, D=127 — the largest case). `crv_types.py`: gold mass spread over **18 module-level distribution constructors** plus the module docstring/`__all__` block (21 lines). Dominant "owner" holds 17% of the mass. No ranking signal picks 18 functions; this is the **distributed-sweep pattern** — the patch applies one systematic edit everywhere. The only levers that touch it: preamble force-include (the 21 module lines) and per-file depth (more spans per already-selected file).

**2. `pydata__xarray-6992`** (Verified, D=95). Gold `Dataset.set_index` (139 lines, rank **59/203**, metric 0.160) loses to `DataVariables.__len__` — a **2-line `len(self._dataset._variables)` stub, rank 1, metric 1.005 (6.3× higher)**. E13's `emptyPrinter` story verbatim, post-adoption: 0.85 barely dents a 42× token ratio. Ten chosen spans already sit inside class `Dataset` (sibling precondition holds), but gold's in-class unseated rank is 25 — a naive sibling boost also fails here.

**3. `sympy__sympy-12489`** (Verified, D=108). Gold `Permutation.__new__` (125 lines, rank 30/82) misses while the engine's chosen span `[926, 952]` starts **one line after gold ends** — the adjacent sibling. Fragmentation on top: 15 distinct owner functions share the file's D-mass (`__new__` holds only 24%). The adjacent-pick + fragmented-mass combination shows why pad-reach measured 0%: locality is *almost* right at the function-boundary level while every actual gold line stays >5 lines away.

**4. `scikit-learn__scikit-learn-25500`** (Lite, D=36, carried over from E7b). Gold `IsotonicRegression.transform` (36 lines, metric 0.1237) loses to module-level `isotonic_regression` (53 lines — **longer than gold**, metric 0.1346, a 1.09× knife-edge). The query names `IsotonicRegression` almost verbatim; the chosen function's name is an exact substring hit and its docstring is saturated with query vocabulary. No exponent flips this (gold has less gain *and* fewer tokens — flip-exp 0.97 > any viable setting is on the wrong side); it is the clean example of the **name-density reversal** class where further length-damping actively hurts.

**5. `astropy__astropy-14369`** (Verified, D=36). Gold file is `cds_parsetab.py` — a **generated parser table** (one candidate block in the whole file). The engine returned only the file header `[1, 9]`, overlapping no function (the E7b "degenerate fallback" pattern); gold lines start at line 30 in the module-level grammar data. Neither function ranking nor sibling logic applies; a first-~30-lines preamble force-include captures a real slice, and the case doubles as a reminder that generated files sit in the corpus as first-class citizens.

## Ranked top-2 implementable signals

**#1 — Module-level / preamble force-include (highest-certainty remaining lever; targets 16% of dominant-file D-mass directly, present in 28/30 files, dominant in 7/30 cases).** Third consecutive mining round to surface it (E13 → E7b → here), still unshipped, and its share grows every time a function-level fix lands because it is the one pattern *no* function-ranking signal can ever reach. Concretely (unchanged from E13): for every file already selected into the bundle, force-include its top-level import/constant/docstring block (first ~20–30 lines or a cheap AST pass-derived preamble span) at near-zero token cost; `astropy-14369`-style degenerate cases suggest also treating "no candidate scored, fell back to file header" as an explicit preamble-include rather than an accident. Blast radius is minimal (only adds small spans to already-selected files; file selection untouched, so the FILE=277/300 invariant is structurally safe) — but per campaign discipline it still gets the full Lite-300 + Verified-406 gate.

**#2 — Anchored-class sibling boost, with eyes open (precondition covers 74% of method-gold contests / 51% of top-30 D-mass; honest naive-boost yield ~9–17% of contests).** The precondition coverage jumped from 42% (E13) to 74% — post-adoption, the engine anchors the right class more often than ever and then picks a sibling stub. But stage-2b shows a bonus that re-ranks siblings by the *same density metric* seats gold in only 2–4 of 12 covered cases; in `Query`/`Dataset`-sized classes the within-class contest is the whole-file contest in miniature. If this ships, it should therefore (i) allocate *depth* (2–3 extra sibling spans per anchored class) rather than re-rank for a single extra pick, and/or (ii) break in-class ties with the one orthogonal signal that improved this round (strict call-graph coupling, 35%) rather than density. Expected value is genuinely positive but a fraction of the 51% headline; gate accordingly and expect partial-fraction gains more than all-or-nothing FUNCTION flips (which also serves the fragmented 11/30 multi-owner cases, where extra in-class depth is the only mechanism that can capture a sweep).

**Explicitly closed by this round's evidence:** (i) **further padding** — 0/30 files have any D-line within 5 (or 10) lines of a chosen span; median gold-function distance 57 lines; (ii) **deeper length exponent** — zero top-30 flips at 0.6/0.7, remaining pool needs ≤0.5, and the combo 0.55 gate already regressed 5.7pp FUNCTION; the two gold-shorter reversal cases show over-damping has real cost; (iii) **name-match weighting** — 26% discrimination, third weak result in a row, and it remains production-regressing per the w_name A/B.

**Honest nulls / drift worth recording:** rare-term possession collapsed 73% → 43% — the adoption selectively rescued the cases where gold held a rare term, so the surviving pool is *lexically flatter* (gold vs chosen) than E13's was; exception-type match stays null (2/7). Both confirm case-mining's recurring lesson: the pool's structure changes under every adopted fix, and last wave's second-best signal is not automatically this wave's.
